My Story

Where It Started

Growing up, I was always the kid who wanted to know how things worked — taking apart electronics, building models, asking "why?" at every turn. That curiosity eventually pointed me toward biomedical engineering, a discipline where the stakes are as high as they get: the end user is a human being.

I chose Virginia Tech for its hands-on research culture and tight-knit engineering community. From day one, I was in labs, on teams, and in competitions — learning that the best solutions come from collaboration, not isolation.

After graduating in 2022, I realized that the skills that made me a good engineer — systems thinking, structured problem-solving, cross-functional communication — translated powerfully into the world of business technology. That realization sent me down a path that's been anything but linear, and I wouldn't have it any other way.
