Overview

This project involved designing and deploying a comprehensive financial analytics dashboard for cross-departmental use. The goal was to replace fragmented spreadsheet-based reporting with a single, interactive source of truth for revenue tracking, expense monitoring, and forecasting.

Dashboard screenshot — replace with your image

Problem

The organization relied on manual Excel reports distributed via email, leading to inconsistent data, delayed insights, and difficulty identifying trends across business units. Leadership needed a real-time view of financial health that could support faster, more informed decision-making.

Approach

  • Conducted stakeholder interviews to define KPIs and reporting requirements across finance, operations, and executive teams.
  • Built a normalized data model in SQL Server, consolidating data from three separate source systems.
  • Designed DAX measures for year-over-year comparisons, rolling averages, and budget variance analysis.
  • Created an interactive Power BI report with drill-through pages, dynamic filtering, and mobile-optimized views.
Data model diagram — replace with your image

Outcome

The dashboard reduced monthly reporting time by approximately 60% and became the primary tool for leadership review meetings. It enabled the team to identify a recurring cost overrun in one department within the first month of deployment, leading to a corrective action that saved an estimated $45K annually.

Reflection

This project reinforced the value of spending time upfront on stakeholder alignment and data architecture. The most impactful dashboards aren't the flashiest — they're the ones people actually use. Clear labeling, sensible defaults, and a tight connection between KPIs and business questions made all the difference.
